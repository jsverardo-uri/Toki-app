# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Additional Team Policies (305Soft Requirements)

*(Yes, this is a one-person team — but we are still holding ourselves accountable.)*

### Technology Use
- No distracted use of technology during meetings (phones away, no unrelated browsing).
- Meetings should remain focused and efficient.

### Decision Making
- Decisions will be made quickly and pragmatically, prioritizing project requirements and deadlines.
- When applicable, decisions will favor simplicity and maintainability.

### Attendance
- Regular check-ins will be scheduled as needed.
- Legitimate absences include illness or emergencies.
- Missed work must be caught up independently and promptly.

### Assignments
- Tasks are self-assigned based on project needs.
- Work must meet course and project standards.
- Incomplete or low-quality work must be revised immediately.

### Participation
- Communication will occur through GitHub, project notes, and development logs.
- Progress and decisions will be documented clearly.
- Work will align with personal strengths while still covering all required areas.

### Meeting Times and Mediums
- Meetings (if applicable) will be self-scheduled based on availability.
- Most coordination will occur asynchronously.

### Agenda and Notes
- Notes will be maintained in project documentation (README, commits, or docs folder).
- Decisions and changes will be recorded for traceability.

### Promptness
- Deadlines are expected to be met on time.
- Delays should be minimized and addressed immediately.

### Conversational Courtesies
- Maintain professional and respectful communication (even if talking to yourself in commit messages).
- Keep documentation clear, concise, and constructive.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
* Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information without permission
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and interactions related to development and communication.
